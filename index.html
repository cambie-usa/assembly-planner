Purchase order recommendations

Order-up-to level = forecasted demand over (lead time + target cover). Net need = order-up-to level − (available + on order). For vendors with a container size set (Vendors tab), individual SKU net-needs are pooled and rounded up to a full container, with any leftover space filled as overstock on your best-selling SKUs — otherwise quantities round up to case packs where defined. "Days of supply" shows how long current stock + incoming POs will last at the current use rate — useful for spotting items you're well covered on, not just ones that need action.

Repacked-goods coverage for affiliate orders (CIN7)

These are the repacked retail-size SKUs (e.g. 600454, 600460) you sell to your affiliate company via CIN7, who then lists them on Amazon. These CIN7 sales are intentionally excluded from raw-material demand — counting them would double-count against the Amazon sales Sellerboard already reports for the same end-customer purchase. This view instead answers a different question: do you have enough finished, already-repacked inventory on hand to keep fulfilling those affiliate orders without a production gap?

How long it takes to repack/produce a fresh batch of these sizes once you decide to run them.

"On hand (domestic)" and "Days of supply" use only your CIN7 warehouse stock and drive the production recommendation — that's what determines whether you can fulfill the next affiliate order. The Amazon FBA+AWD columns are shown for context only (how much runway your affiliate has left at Amazon) and deliberately don't affect the production math, since that stock has already left your warehouse.

Total pounds forecast, by flavor

Every channel a flavor is sold through, converted into a single "lbs of candy" number per flavor — bulk bags sold directly (Amazon + CIN7) and every repacked retail size (1 lb, 2 lb, 8 oz bags etc, Amazon only) all traced back through the BOM to the base bulk bag and summed. This reuses the exact same demand figures driving your PO recommendations, just regrouped by flavor and expressed in pounds instead of split across dozens of SKUs and units. The monthly table below uses SoStocked's real seasonality where available and extends to a full rolling 12 months with a flat run-rate beyond that; B2B/CIN7 demand is averaged evenly across all 12 months since there's no monthly B2B history yet.
